# Discovery: PulseBoard

## Research Questions
- How do planners currently learn about stockout risk today?
- What signals do they trust enough to act on immediately?
- Where does the 8-10 hours a week of reconciliation actually go?

## Key Insights
- Planners check 3-5 separate spreadsheets before every S&OP meeting [source: inputs/interviews/priya_planner_2026-07-10.md]
- Trust in automated alerts is low because past tools cried wolf on noisy SKUs [source: inputs/customer_feedback/q2_support_tickets_reconciliation.md]
- The real bottleneck is reconciling unit-of-measure mismatches between ERP and demand-planning exports [source: inputs/interviews/priya_planner_2026-07-10.md]
- Mid-size manufacturers average 6-9 hours/week per planner on manual reconciliation industry-wide, and trust in automated alerts is the top adoption blocker [source: inputs/secondary_research/gartner_supply_chain_visibility_2026.md]

## Personas
- **Priya, Senior Demand Planner**: Owns forecast accuracy for 40 SKUs across 4 distribution centers, currently exports to Excel every Monday
- **Marcus, Ops Manager**: Runs the weekly S&OP review, needs a one-page risk summary he can trust without re-checking the math

## Opportunity Areas
- Automated reconciliation across ERP and demand-planning exports
- Trustworthy, low-noise risk surfacing tied to real customer impact
- A single pre-meeting summary view for the weekly S&OP review
