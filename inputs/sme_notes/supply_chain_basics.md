# SME notes: Mid-size manufacturing supply chain operations

- **SME**: Former VP of Supply Chain, 18 years in industrial manufacturing
- **Date captured**: 2026-07-08

## Domain vocabulary
- **S&OP (Sales & Operations Planning)**: the weekly/monthly cross-functional
  meeting where demand forecasts, supply plans, and inventory risk are
  reconciled - the meeting PulseBoard's summary view is built for.
- **Stockout**: running out of a SKU needed to fulfill a confirmed order.
- **Expedite shipment**: an emergency, above-standard-cost shipment used to
  cover a stockout after the fact - the cost this product aims to reduce.
- **SKU**: stock-keeping unit - a specific trackable inventory item.

## Regulatory / compliance constraints
None specific to this domain beyond standard data-handling practices for
customer order data - no PII/PHI-equivalent regulatory regime applies here.

## What "good" looks like by industry standard
Best-in-class planners in this segment target under 2 hours/week on manual
reconciliation and near-zero surprise stockouts (i.e., every stockout was
flagged with enough lead time to expedite calmly rather than scramble).

## Common failure modes in this domain
- Alert fatigue from noisy, low-precision automated systems - once trust is
  lost, planners revert to manual checking even if the tool is objectively
  faster
- Systems that surface "risk" without a clear recommended action get
  ignored during time-pressured S&OP prep
