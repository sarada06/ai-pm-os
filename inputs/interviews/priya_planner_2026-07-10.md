# Interview: Priya Nair, Senior Demand Planner

- **Date**: 2026-07-10
- **Method**: video call
- **Segment**: Demand planner, 3-10 distribution center manufacturers
- **Interviewer**: PM

## Context
Priya owns forecast accuracy for 40 SKUs across 4 distribution centers at a
mid-size industrial parts manufacturer. Shadowed her weekly reconciliation
routine.

## Key quotes
- "By the time I see the stockout in the ERP report, the customer's already called asking where their order is"
- "I don't trust the automated alerts anymore - too many false alarms on slow-moving SKUs"

## Pain points observed
- Manually cross-references 3 spreadsheets and 1 ERP export every Monday morning
- Unit-of-measure mismatches between systems cause silent errors that take hours to catch
- No single view of "what's about to go wrong" before it becomes a customer issue

## Current workaround
Exports ERP data to Excel every Monday, cross-references against a shared
team spreadsheet, and manually flags anything that looks off before the
Tuesday S&OP meeting.

## Follow-ups / open questions
Would she trust a new automated system after being burned by noisy alerts
before? What would rebuild that trust?
